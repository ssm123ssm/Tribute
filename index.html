Timeline of Ed Sheeran

  • 1991 - Ed Sheeran was born on February 17, 1991, in Halifax, West Yorkshire, in the United Kingdom.
  • 2002 - Sheeran met singer-songwriter Damien Rice backstage at one of Rice’s shows, and the young musician found added inspiration. Sheeran started writing songs as told by Rice.
  • 2005 - Sheeran headed to London for the summer. Thinking he could find gigs in the big city, Sheeran left home with his guitar and a backpack full of clothes, and his musical career took flight.
  • 2006 - Ed released his first two albums; a self-titled record and 'Want Some?'
  • 2011 - Ed Sheeran headed to US and he found a new fan in Jamie Foxx, who asked Sheeran to appear on his Sirius radio show. Soon after, in January 2011, Sheeran released yet another EP, his last as an independent artist. Without any promotion, the record reached No. 2 on the iTunes chart, and he signed on with Atlantic Records that same month.
  • 2013 - Sheeran began co-writing songs with bigger artists, such as One Direction and Taylor Swift.
  • 2014 - His next album, x, appeared, debuting at No. 1 in the U.S. and U.K. The project featured three Top 10 singles—"Don't," "Photograph" and "Thinking Out Loud"—with the latter winning the Grammy for Song of the Year and Best Pop Solo Performance in 2016.
  • 2017 - he released two singles from the album, "Shape of You" and "Castle on the Hill," which debuted at number one and number six, respectively, on the Billboard Hot 100, Sheeran released ÷ in March 2017, and announced a 2017 world tour.
